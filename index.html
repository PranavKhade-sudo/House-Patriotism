About the Tiranga

The Tiranga — orange (saffron), white, and green with the Ashoka Chakra — is India's national flag. Saffron stands for courage and sacrifice, white for peace and truth, green for faith and chivalry, and the 24-spoke Ashoka Chakra for progress and righteousness.

The Tiranga unites people across languages, regions, and religions. It represents India's long journey for freedom, equality, and democracy.

Important Social Reformers

Raja Ram Mohan Roy

Championed social reform in the 19th century; fought against Sati and promoted education and women's rights.

Jyotirao Phule & Savitribai Phule

Pioneers of education for girls and lower-caste communities in Maharashtra; started schools and fought caste discrimination.

Dr. B. R. Ambedkar

Architect of the Indian Constitution; worked lifelong for social justice and the rights of marginalized communities.

Mahatma Gandhi

Leader of India's independence movement who used nonviolent civil disobedience and fought for social reforms.
